# Question Bank — Behavioral / Cultural-Fit / Soft-Skill

This file is the consolidated question taxonomy synthesized from the corpus. Each family lists representative English and Spanish entries. The `playbook` mode selects from these families based on the JD; the `mock-interview` mode drills from them.

## A. Self-introduction & narrative

The opening family. Establish identity, motivation, and value proposition in 60–90 seconds. Every playbook produces an elevator pitch in this family.

- Tell me about yourself. / Cuéntame un poco sobre ti.
- Why this company? / ¿Por qué quieres trabajar en esta empresa?
- Why this role? / ¿Por qué te gustaría trabajar en X puesto?
- Why should we hire you? / ¿Por qué deberíamos escogerte a ti?
- Why are you changing jobs? / ¿Por qué quieres cambiar de trabajo?
- What's your elevator pitch (30-second professional summary)?
- What are your greatest achievements? / ¿Cuáles han sido tus mayores logros?

**Coaching (from *preguntas y respuestas entrevista.pdf*)**: For "cuéntame un poco sobre ti" — do NOT recite the CV; do NOT start with childhood; do NOT ramble. DO lead with one professional identity sentence, then one relevance-to-the-role sentence, then one why-this-company sentence. Max 90 seconds.

## B. Strengths & weaknesses (trap questions)

High-trap family. Designed to expose negativity, dishonesty, or generic AI-level platitudes.

- What are your strengths / 3 virtues? / ¿Dime tus 3 mayores virtudes?
- What is your greatest weakness? / ¿Cuál es tu mayor debilidad?
- How would you describe yourself in three words? / ¿Cómo te definirías en tres palabras?
- How do you react to criticism? / ¿Cómo reaccionas ante las críticas?
- How do you handle stress? / ¿Cómo reaccionas ante situaciones de estrés?

**Coaching**: For weaknesses — a real weakness paired with a mitigation is strongest (per *Cracking the Coding Interview*). "I'm a perfectionist" is a forbidden answer. For strengths — pick exactly 3, with a one-line specific evidence per strength (no more, no less).

## C. Behavioral / experience (STAR-eliciting)

Routed through STAR + Nugget First. Every answer should lead with a nugget, then walk Situation → Task → Action → Result.

- Tell me about a time you had a tight deadline and how you met it. If you didn't meet it, how did you handle the situation? (Spanish source)
- Describe a situation where you used persuasion to successfully convince someone to see things your way. (*Interviewology*)
- Step by step, using a real-life example, tell me how you delegated responsibilities on a project and how you handled it. (*Interviewology*)
- Walk me through your biggest failure, what you learned, and how you dealt with it. (*Interviewology*)
- Tell me about a challenging project you worked on. (*Frontend Handbook*)
- Tell me about a time you disagreed with a technical decision. (*Frontend Handbook*)
- Tell me about a time you had a challenging interaction with a teammate. (*Cracking* S.A.R. example)
- What kind of disagreements have you had with colleagues/managers and how did you resolve them? (*metainterview*)
- How do you operate in an ambiguous, fast-changing environment? (*metainterview*)
- How do you keep your code clean? (*Frontend Handbook*)

## D. Leadership & teamwork

- Can you lead teams? / ¿Te ves capaz de liderar equipos?
- What role do you take in a team? / ¿Cómo sos a la hora de trabajar en equipo, qué rol tomas?
- How do you demonstrate your impact / are you self-directed in reaching goals? (*metainterview*)
- How do you tailor communications to different audiences / cross-functional partners? (*metainterview*)
- How have you approached improving your skills? (*metainterview*)

## E. Cultural fit & company research

These are most accurate when the `playbook` has additional company context. If the `job-application` skill's `company-research` mode has been run, that output should feed these answers.

- What do you know about us and the role? / ¿Qué sabes de la empresa y el puesto?
- What do you like (and dislike) about your current job? (trap)
- What do you like about our company? / ¿Qué es lo que te gusta de nuestra empresa? (trap)
- How long would you stay with us? / ¿Cuánto tiempo te quedarías con nosotros? (trap)
- What are you looking for? / ¿Qué es lo que estás buscando? (trap)
- Are you in other selection processes? / ¿Estás en algún otro proceso de selección? (trap)
- What makes our company better than others? / ¿Qué crees que hace nuestra empresa mejor que otras?
- What is your background/education? / ¿Háblame de tu formación académica?
- Do you have enough training for this role? / ¿Crees que tienes la suficiente formación para el puesto?

**Gap-handling variants** (when the candidate has a CV gap):
- Why have you been unemployed so long? / ¿Por qué has estado tanto tiempo sin trabajar?
- You don't have enough experience. / No tienes la suficiente experiencia.
- You have too much experience. / Tienes demasiada experiencia.

### Canonical 8-question cultural sequence (from interview-ready.md)

1. Tell me about yourself.
2. Why are you interested in this role/company?
3. Tell me about a time you demonstrated [VALUE_X] — values bank: Ownership, Customer Obsession, Empathy, Technical Excellence, Resilience, Focus on Impact, Be Bold (fall back to this bank when company doesn't publish values).
4. Tell me about a time you disagreed with leadership.
5. Tell me about a time you worked with an underperforming teammate.
6. What's the hardest technical challenge you've solved?
7. Tell me about a project that failed.
8. What questions do you have for us?

This canonical recruiter-validated sequence is useful when company-research is unavailable and the playbook needs a concrete ordering.

## F. Questions to ask the interviewer

The `playbook` always produces at least 3 candidate questions. Mix styles: genuine (what brought the interviewer to the company), insightful (why a specific technical/product decision), and passion (opportunities to learn X).

- What brought you to this company, and what's been most challenging? (*Cracking*)
- Why did the product choose protocol X over Y? (insightful, per *Cracking*)
- What opportunities are there to learn about scalability? (passion, per *Cracking*)
- Why are you hiring for this role? / ¿Por qué estáis buscando un candidato? (Spanish)
- What's the ideal candidate profile? / ¿Qué tipo de perfil buscáis? (Spanish)
- What are the promotion possibilities? / ¿Qué posibilidades de ascenso hay? (Spanish)
- What training options exist? / ¿Qué opciones de formación hay? (Spanish)
- What retains employees here? / ¿Qué es lo que retiene a los trabajadores? (Spanish)
- How do you evaluate performance and compensation? / ¿Cómo evaluáis el desempeño y retribución? (Spanish)
- How many years has the company operated? / ¿Cuántos años lleva funcionando? (Spanish)
- What's your competitive advantage? / ¿Qué ventaja competitiva tenéis? (Spanish)

## G. Trait-keyed behavioral probes (Avature)

A second-axis bank grouped by assessed trait (not interview phase), sourced from Avature's internal rubric. Each trait has a one-line assessment cue and 3-5 question stems. Pull from G when the JD names a specific competency.

**Adaptability / Flexibility**
Assessment cue: How candidates adjust to changing requirements, contexts, and unexpected conditions.
- Tell me about a time you had to adapt to a difficult situation. What problems did you face?
- Have you ever had a project that changed drastically mid-flight? How did you handle it?
- Tell me about a time you adjusted to a colleague's working style to complete a project.
- Tell me about a time you had to adapt to a technology or procedure you disagreed with.
- Did you ever take on a task outside your routine? What results did you get?

**Ownership**
Assessment cue: Whether candidates take responsibility for outcomes and understand the purpose of their duties.
- Tell me about a project that failed. What did you do about it?
- Tell me about a project you take pride in. What was your input?
- Have you ever missed a deadline? What happened and what did you do?
- You're blocked on a key project waiting on a colleague. What would you do?

**Resourcefulness**
Assessment cue: Ability to solve problems without guidance, policies, or help.
- Give me an example of the most creative solution you've come up with for a difficult problem.
- Tell me about a time you had to solve a problem with no policies, guidelines, or superiors to help.
- What types of tasks do you solve on your own vs escalate to your manager?
- Tell me about a tight deadline with almost no collaboration from peers. What decisions did you take?

**Strategic Mindset / Awareness**
Assessment cue: How candidates evaluate multiple options and make decisions with long-term impact in context.
- Think about a time you had several directions for a project. Walk me through your decision process.
- Tell me about an important decision. How many factors did you consider?
- What steps do you follow to study a problem before deciding?
- Describe a time you made a quick decision with incomplete information.
- How do you know a decision has long-term impact?

**Fast learner**
Assessment cue: Capacity to make analogies and connect concepts quickly during onboarding.
- Tell me about the last time you had to learn a new task. How did you go about it?
- Can you recount a time you felt ill-equipped for an assignment? How did you overcome it?
- Share something you learned on the job and applied to future tasks.
- Tell me about a time you had to learn something new in a short amount of time.

**Listening**
Assessment cue: Whether candidates incorporate information shared earlier and bring meaningful input.
- Tell me about a meeting with stakeholders holding different opinions. How did you grasp each position?
- Tell me about receiving feedback from a leader. What opportunity areas did you identify and act on?

**Abstract thinking / Creativity**
Assessment cue: Ability to imagine multiple scenarios and solve problems out of the box.
- How would you describe the color yellow to someone who can't see?
- How would you explain your job to an alien?
- Tell me about a complex problem that involved several variables and possible outcomes.

**Curiosity**
Assessment cue: How eager they are to research, learn, and share insights beyond the surface.
- What have you done to improve your skills lately?
- Tell me about something you learned in the last 6 months.
- Is there anything you research outside working hours? What and why?
- What additional information do you seek to add value to your job?

**Patience / Tenacity**
Assessment cue: Staying engaged through extended effort and handling stubborn people.
- Tell me about a situation that really tested your patience. What is your approach with stubborn people?
- Describe a project you worked on over an extended period. How did you stay engaged?

**Personable**
Assessment cue: Energy level, rapport-building, mood — do they engage socially.
- Tell me about a time you had to persuade someone to see your side.
- Tell me about a time you turned around an angry or dissatisfied customer.

**Empathy**
Assessment cue: Whether they put themselves in others' shoes and act on what they hear.
- Do other people come to you for counsel?
- Tell me about a disagreement with a team member. How did you solve your differences?
- What is your approach when a client asks for unrealistic deadlines?
- A teammate is upset. What is your first reaction?

**Proactivity**
Assessment cue: Whether they carry out initiatives and find new solutions to problems.
- Have you ever brought new ideas to change an existing process? Give an example.
- What would you do if you made a mistake no one else noticed?
- What would you do if asked to perform a task you've never done before?
- What professional accomplishment are you most proud of and how did you achieve it?
- You were looking for something and the data ran out. What did you do?

**Mature**
Assessment cue: How many angles of a topic they consider and whether they complain without action.
- Tell me about a time you felt defeated. How did you respond?
- Tell me about the most important decision you've made. How did you gather information?
- Tell me about a time you received negative feedback.

**Confidence**
Assessment cue: Body language, composure under discomfort, ability to support ideas with evidence.
- Tell me about a time you disagreed with something at your workplace. What did you do?
- What do you solve on your own vs validate with your boss?
- Tell me about proposing a solution when a coworker proposed another. What was the result?
- You're leading a presentation and someone asks a question you can't answer. What do you do?
- Describe the most difficult client you worked with and how you managed the conversation.

**Organizational-Prioritizing**
Assessment cue: How they choose between competing tasks and manage time in critical situations.
- Tell me about a time you were particularly effective at prioritizing tasks and completing on schedule.
- How do you organize your work today?
- What would you do if you had to deal with a problem last minute?
- What would you do if given 5 important tasks but only time for 2?
- When organizing a project, what steps do you take?

**Coaching note on graded answers**: `assets/notion-exports/interview-ready.md` (lines ~1239-1423) contains 4-tier graded-answer exemplars (Strong Yes / Yes / No / Strong No with Green/Red flag labels) for several canonical questions including "Tell me about yourself" and "Why are you interested in this position?" — useful as a coaching artifact during `playbook` and `mock-interview` modes.

## Selection logic (skill-internal)

| Skill mode | Families pulled |
|---|---|
| `personality-strategy` | A (pitch), B (modulated by archetype) |
| `playbook` | A (elevator pitch), B, C (3–5 selected by JD), D, E (with company context), F (3 generated) |
| `mock-interview` | C and B heavy; A once at session start; E with one trap drill; F one closing round; mix C-family drills with at least one drill pulled from G |
| `playbook` (JD names a competency) | Pull from G/<trait> above the A-F rotation |

When a JD is provided to the `playbook`, scan it for keyword signals and elevate the C-family questions whose domain they imply (leadership signals → "delegated responsibilities", conflict signals → "disagreements with managers", ambiguity signals → "ambiguous environment"). When combination with the `job-application` skill is available, use its company-research output to specialize E-family questions.
